Our earlier work on Loa-loa modelling developed a model for the cumulative distribution function of intensity of Loa Loa infection based on community-level prevalence from rapid diagnostic tests. This allows us to estimate the proportion of the community whose parasite counts exceed certain policy relevant infection levels (for example 8,000 or 30,000 microfilariae/ml) which correspond to high and very high risk of severe adverse events when the individuals are treated with drugs used in the mass administration programme to eliminate Lymphatic Filariasis.

In an extension of this project we will be updating our risk map by incorporating the latest data from both rapid diagnostic test results and blood sample tests. The model also includes geographic covariates that give a broad indication of habitat suitability for Loa-loa infection.
