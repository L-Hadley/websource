We believe that social network analysis is a neglected aspect of epidemiological studies. Network analysis can highlight subtle details within a population, details which can have a disproportionate effect on disease dynamics within the network.

Consideration of the topological properties of the connectivity of the network may help preferentially select people for vaccination, for example.

This work is in collaboration with Rachel Isba of the Medical School.
